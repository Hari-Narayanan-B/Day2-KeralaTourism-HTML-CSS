Alappuzha

Alappuzha, also known as Alleppey, is a district in Kerala famous for its backwaters, houseboats, and unique cultural festivals. Often referred to as the "Venice of the East," Alappuzha is a popular tourist destination that attracts visitors from around the world.

The district is renowned for its picturesque backwaters, where visitors can enjoy serene boat rides amidst lush greenery and coconut palms. The houseboat cruises are a major attraction, offering a glimpse into the traditional lifestyle of the local people.

Alappuzha is also known for its vibrant festivals, particularly the Nehru Trophy Boat Race, which takes place on the Punnamada Lake and showcases the rich cultural heritage of the region. The district's unique cuisine, featuring seafood and traditional Kerala dishes, adds to its charm.

In addition to its natural beauty, Alappuzha is home to several historical landmarks, including the Alappuzha Lighthouse and the ancient temples that reflect the region's rich history and architectural heritage.

With its stunning landscapes, cultural richness, and warm hospitality, Alappuzha remains one of the most enchanting districts in Kerala.

Tourist Attractions

  • Backwaters of Alappuzha
  • Houseboat Cruises
  • Nehru Trophy Boat Race
  • Alappuzha Lighthouse
  • Krishnapuram Palace
  • Pathiramanal Island
  • Ambalappuzha Sree Krishna Temple
