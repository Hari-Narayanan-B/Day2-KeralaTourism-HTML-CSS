Kollam

Kollam, also known as Quilon, is a city on the Malabar Coast of Kerala, India. It is one of the oldest ports on the Arabian Sea and has a rich history dating back to ancient times. Kollam is famous for its cashew processing and coir industries, contributing significantly to the economy of Kerala.

The district is known for its beautiful backwaters, with Ashtamudi Lake being one of the most prominent attractions. The lake is a UNESCO-recognized wetland and is famous for its houseboat cruises, offering a unique experience of Kerala's natural beauty.

Kollam is also home to several historical landmarks, including the Thangassery Lighthouse, which offers panoramic views of the Arabian Sea, and the ancient Portuguese church, which reflects the colonial history of the region.

In addition to its natural beauty and historical significance, Kollam hosts various cultural festivals, including the famous Kollam Pooram, which showcases the rich traditions and vibrant culture of the region.

The district is well-connected by road, rail, and air, making it easily accessible for tourists. With its blend of history, culture, and natural beauty, Kollam is a must-visit destination in Kerala.
