Kottayam

Kottayam is a city in the Indian state of Kerala, known for its rich cultural heritage and educational institutions. It is often referred to as the 'Land of Letters' due to its significant contributions to literature and education in Kerala.

The district is famous for its rubber plantations, which are a major source of income for the local population. Kottayam is also known for its scenic landscapes, with lush green hills and beautiful backwaters that attract tourists from all over the world.

One of the key attractions in Kottayam is the Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary, which is home to a variety of migratory birds and offers a serene environment for nature lovers. The Vembanad Lake, the longest lake in India, is another popular destination for houseboat rides and water sports.

Kottayam is also known for its educational institutions, including the prestigious Mahatma Gandhi University and several renowned colleges that contribute to the city's reputation as an educational hub.

The district celebrates various festivals with great enthusiasm, including the famous Onam festival, which showcases the rich cultural traditions of Kerala.

Tourist Attractions

  • Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary
  • Vembanad Lake
  • Thirunakkara Mahadeva Temple
  • Vaikom Mahadeva Temple
  • Elaveezhapoonchira
  • Pathiramanal Island
  • St. Mary's Church
