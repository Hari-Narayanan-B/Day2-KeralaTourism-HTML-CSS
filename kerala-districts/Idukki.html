Idukki

Idukki is a picturesque district located in the Western Ghats of Kerala, known for its stunning hills, wildlife sanctuaries, and the famous Idukki Dam. The district is characterized by its lush greenery, rich biodiversity, and breathtaking landscapes, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

The Idukki Dam, one of the highest arch dams in Asia, is a significant landmark in the district. It is built across the Periyar River and is a major source of hydroelectric power for the state. The dam and its surrounding areas offer scenic views and opportunities for trekking and exploration.

Idukki is also home to several wildlife sanctuaries, including the Idukki Wildlife Sanctuary and the Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary, which are rich in flora and fauna. These sanctuaries provide a habitat for various species, including elephants, tigers, and numerous bird species, making it a haven for wildlife enthusiasts.

The district is known for its spice plantations, particularly cardamom and pepper, which contribute significantly to the local economy. Visitors can explore these plantations and learn about the cultivation and processing of spices.

Idukki's natural beauty is complemented by its cultural heritage, with several temples and festivals celebrated throughout the year. The district's serene environment and tranquil atmosphere make it an ideal destination for those seeking peace and relaxation.
